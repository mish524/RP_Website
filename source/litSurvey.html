Literature Survey

The health sector is known as a major sector in any country. Looking back from the present to the past, we can see incredible growth in the healthcare sector, with the latest developments in the field of technology. But sadly, the health sector in Sri Lanka is not up to date. A good Health Management Information System (HMIS) is necessary to manage a health organization effectively and efficiently. In the health sector an extensive demand for Electronic Medical Record (EMR) system is available currently in worldwide as well as in local health care providers. World Health Organization (WHO) introduced a health information system to Sri Lanka two decades ago but that is not up and running by now because it is not possible and customizable to Sri Lanka. By analyzing existing systems as open Medical Record System (open MRS) which is a patient-centric record system focusing on developing world where malaria, tuberculosis, and acquired immune deficiency syndrome (AIDS)effect humans' lives and Health Information Management System (HHIMS) an open-source medical record software developed for use in Sri Lankan hospitals. By keeping a good health management information system with correct data enables physicians to make the most correct decisions and to supply the most effective treatment to patients. The two leading challengers in the health sector are the low level of technical knowledge and the complexity of the health sector. By analyzing current challenges and existing health management systems such as openMRS and e-Hospital in Dompe, we introduced a new system called Smart Patient Care System (SPCS) for managing inward patients in more advanced and effective ways. Our system consists of four modules. The automated Electronic Bed Head Ticket module is the main part in our system, followed by the Inward Patient managing module, Medical Chatbot module, and medical predictions module related to the electronic bed head ticket.
